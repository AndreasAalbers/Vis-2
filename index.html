Population Density around the world

Take a moment to embark on this journey across the globe through the lens of the Choropleth Map, portraying Population Density (per kilometre squared). The darker shades of blue indicate a higher density count, providing an immediate visual indicator of which parts of the globe hold a larger population.

Interactive features: hover over each country to view their specific details and use the density count slider which will allow you to focus on specific regions with magnified data values.

Note: Grey areas indicate countries with invalid data.

Infant Mortality Rate vs GDP per capita

Infant Mortality Rate and GDP per capita are two distinct but interconnected measures used to assess a countries overall well-being. The Infant Mortality Rate, expressed as the number of infant deaths per 1,000 live births, provides insight into the quality of healthcare, sanitation, and social conditions in a nation.

GDP per capita represents the average income per person in a country, which is indicative of its economic prosperity. There is often an inverse relationship between these two measures; higher GDP per capita tends to be associated with lower infant mortality rates.

Interactive features: hover over each bubble to view specific details. Use the ‘GDP’ and ‘Mortality’ sliders to focus on specific regions with magnified data values. Use the drop down menu to select a specific continent.

A look into unemployment rates

Unemployment rates are another important concept which indicate how well a country is travelling. Unemployment is a measure of workers who want to work but are unable to find jobs.

By having a high unemployment rate, the country will have a lower economic output. Furthermore, a very low unemployment rate can also have negative consequences such as inflation and reduced productivity.

Countries such as South Africa and Iraq can be seen to have much higher rates when compared to other countries such as Vietnam and Ethiopia.

Which countries pollute the most?

CO2 (carbon dioxide) emissions per capita are measured as the total amount of CO2 divided by the total population. Countries which gain energy from renewables have much lower CO2 emissions compared to countries which do not. Countries which are rich in oil are also high due to oil refining.

Furthermore, some of the richer countries have high CO2 emissions due to imports from other countries. Canada had the highest CO2 emissions per capita at 14.86 metric tons while India had the lowest at 1.895.

Interactive features: hover over each slice of the chart to view specific details on each selected country.

Note: t CO2/cap = metric tons of carbon dioxide emissions per capita

Minimum Wage across the globe

Minimum wage is the lowest legally mandated hourly wage that employers are required to pay their workers. It is a fundamental labor policy tool used by governments to ensure that workers receive a fair and liveable income. It helps safeguard workers from exploitative labor practices, reduces income inequality, and provides a basic standard of living.

Interactive features: hover over each country to view their specific details and use the wage slider which will allow you to focus on specific regions with magnified data values.

Note: Grey areas indicate countries with invalid data.
